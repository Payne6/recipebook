Chicken Cacciatore

  • 1 onion
  • 1 leek
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • Pancetta
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/2 butternut squash
  • 100g chestnut mushrooms
  • 1 stock pot
  • 2 x 400g tins plum tomatoes
  • 250ml red wine
  • 8 chicken thighs
  • 12 black olives
  • Mixed dried herbs
  • 1 tbsp tomato and garlic puree
  • Cayenne pepper
  • Rice

Preheat oven to 190C. place large ovenproof pan on medium heat. Peel onion and cut into eighths. Slice leek, pancetta, rosemary and garlic. Place rosemary, garlic and bay leaves in pan with oil. Stir for 2 mins, then add garlic, onion and leek. Cook for 10 mins, stirring regularly. Chop squash into bite-size chunks, leaving skin on, then add to pan with mushrooms. Add chicken. Pour in wine and reduce slightly. Add tomatoes, breaking them up, along with mixed herbs, cayenne pepper and stock pot. Half-fill each tin with water, swirl, then pour water into pan. Stir, then add olives. Bring to a gentle simmer then transfer to oven to cook for 1 hour or until chicken is falling off the bone. Season and serve with rice.
